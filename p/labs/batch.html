Optional: Submit Your Labs in Batch Mode

Though it is not written as a rule, because you signed up for this honor-track course, you are expected to work ahead of lab schedule (e.g. 2 weeks in advance) to learn the material well in advance, especially if you are not familiar with QT. This will help you to equip with the basic skillsets to complete the projects which are larger compared with those in COMP1004.

On the other hand, because the lab assignments are very straightforward, you may finish it within 3 weeks in a batch, and demo it once and for all. The batch mode is entirely an option. If you pick this mode (which you may not), then,

  1. Do all the lab assignments. (Note: We do not expect students to ask many questions if they opt for the batch mode. If a student has difficulty working on his/her own, s/he should attend the lab each week.)
  2. Zip your source codes (.cpp and .h) and makefile into a single zip file called lab.zip.
  3. Upload the zipfile via CASS on or before Mar 4, 2013. Warning: If your submitted lab fails to compile, your lab will be zero (as no late policy applies to labs).
  4. Then, send an email to yxiaoab@cs to notify the TA that you have submitted your lab in batch mode. The email must be sent on or before the above deadline.
  5. An appointment will be made with you to demo your labs. The demo will last for 45minutes, where you have to convince the TA that you have thorough understanding of the lab assignments (and therefore we use it to verify if students copy labs from each other).
After Feb 28, no batch mode will be accepted (this means you have to attend and demo your labs to your TA to earn the credits).

If you pick the batch mode, and are successful in demonstrating that your labs satisfy all requirements as specified, you score the lab in full and may skip all the comp2012h labs for the entire spring semester! Before you do, though, you need to attend the labs to score the lab assignment. .
